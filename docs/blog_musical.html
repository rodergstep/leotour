Musical Lviv

Inspiration for article is Stanyslav Lyudkevych

Tour Guide — Veronika Hondarenko
Posted on April 19, 2017

The first written mention about Lviv is dated 1256, that history the city has more than 750 years. The city, which was founded at the intersection of profitable trade routes developed and flourished, becoming one of the main centers of medieval Europe. Throughout its history, under the authority of European countries like Poland, Austria-Hungary and Russia, on the Lions went through each part of culture and knowledge and eventually become a pearl of architecture and a great center of scientific, spiritual and artistic life.

Indisputable evidence of the cultural wealth of the city there are many theaters, concert halls, art groups, and conducting in numerous festivals and art events.

Franz Xaver Mozart Franz Xaver Mozart In diverse cultural life of the city has always occupied a significant place music. In 1796, conductor and composer Jozef Elsner, a future teacher of Frederic Chopin, founded in Lviv Music Academy. In 1826 WA Mozart's son Franz Xaver founded the Society of St. Tsetsiliyi (with choir singing and Institute). These two musical institutions were able to unite the best performing forces for cultural and educational live performances and became the prototype of important musical organization of the Austrian province - Galician Musical Society.

From the musical history of the city the names of prominent musicians and actors as Johan Rukhaber (1799-1876), a pupil of Chopin - Karol Mikula (1821-1897), Mieczyslaw Soltys (1863-1929), Rudolf Schwarz (1834- 1899), Denis Sichynskyi Stanislav Nyevyadomskyy. In the musical history of the city also left its mark figures such as singers A.Didur, S.Krushelnytska, M.Mentsynskyy, O.Myshuha; pianists: L.Myuntser, M.Rozental, E.Shtoyerman; cellists: B.Berezhnytskyy, D.Danchovskyy, P.Pshenychka which, no doubt, made a significant contribution to the treasury of world music history.

Separately, we must mention Karol (Nicholas) Savitsky (1792-1850), who began his career violin masters in Ukraine and moved to Vienna, received deserved recognition among artists and musicians violin. Famous is the review of Paganini Savitsky, published in the local press in 1828, which he called his "extraordinary genius and given the skill of making new violins and given perfect restorations of antique string stringed instruments."

The uniqueness of the city is recognized by UNESCO.
